AI vs. Human Knowledge Work

February 2026 — Profession-level displacement risk analysis · GDPval Expert Preferences × BLS Compensation Data

Governing Thought

Even the best AI model (Claude Opus 4.1) wins only 47.6% of expert evaluations across 44 professions — humans still hold the overall edge. But in the $1.4T+ of compensation where AI win rates exceed 50%, displacement has already begun. Gen/Ops Managers ($477B, 67%), Software Developers ($239B, 70%), and Sales Managers ($97B, 79%) face genuine disruption. Meanwhile, Accountants ($135B, 24%) and Financial Managers ($148B, 32%) remain firmly in human territory — a dramatic reversal from conventional wisdom. The data reveals: AI displaces execution-heavy roles first; judgment-heavy professions retain their human advantage.

Key Findings — Profession-Level Displacement Risk
1

Gen/Ops Managers ($477B, 67% win rate) is the single largest AI displacement target in the economy

So what: At 67% AI win rate, this role sits firmly in the "AI Advantage" zone — and it alone represents more at-risk compensation than entire sectors. AI handles the knowledge half (analytics, forecasting, reporting) while the physical half (floor presence, team leadership) creates a partial moat. Expect 30-40% productivity gains and significant headcount pressure.
Claude leads with 67% win rate; GPT-5 follows at 57%. These are Retail Trade roles requiring both strategic analysis and physical operations management — the hybrid nature means AI becomes a copilot first, then gradually absorbs the knowledge work component.
2

The Professional Services myth: Accountants ($135B, 24%), Financial Managers ($148B, 32%) are NOT at high risk

So what: Contrary to popular narrative, AI does NOT yet outperform human experts in these judgment-heavy professions. Combined $392B in compensation (including Project Managers at $109B, 42%) sits below the 50% win rate line. Regulatory complexity, professional judgment, and client relationships create durable human advantages that current AI models cannot overcome.
Accountants: Claude leads at just 24% — meaning human experts win 76% of blind evaluations. Financial Managers: Claude leads at 32%. Even Lawyers ($137B) at 46% remain in human territory. The "AI will replace white-collar professionals" narrative is not yet supported by expert preference data.
3

Sales is ground zero for AI displacement: Sales Managers ($97B, 79%) lead all large professions

So what: Sales Managers ($97B, 79%), Sales Reps Wholesale ($103B, 68%), and Retail Supervisors ($58B, 59%) — combined $258B with the highest concentration of above-60% win rates. Wholesale Trade is the most AI-vulnerable sector by profession-level data. CRM + AI copilots handle prospecting, pipeline management, and follow-ups at superhuman levels.
GPT-5 leads Sales Managers at 79% — the highest win rate among any profession exceeding $50B in compensation. The data shows AI excels at structured, repetitive sales processes. Relationship-dependent roles persist longer but pure transactional sales face immediate displacement.
4

Software Developers ($239B, 70% win rate) — the second-largest at-risk compensation pool

So what: With Claude and o3 both winning 70% of expert evaluations, software development is a confirmed displacement target. But even here, AI loses 30% of the time — complex architecture, cross-system debugging, and domain expertise remain human advantages. The displacement is real but not total.
GPT-4o scores only 30%, showing massive model quality variance. The gap between frontier models (Claude/o3 at 70%) and previous generation (GPT-4o at 30%) means displacement speed depends on which AI tools organizations adopt. Early adopters of frontier models gain 2-3× productivity advantage.
5

Healthcare is a tale of two halves: $323B fortress + $119B contested zone

So what: Registered Nurses ($323B, 37%) are firmly human-dominant — physical patient care creates an impenetrable moat. But Med/Health Services Managers ($78B, 65%) and Nurse Practitioners ($41B, 56%) face real AI competition in their knowledge-work components. The "$400B healthcare fortress" is actually a $323B fortress with a $119B contested zone where AI is gaining ground.
Registered Nurses: best AI model (Claude) wins only 37% — humans dominate in bedside care, clinical judgment, and patient interaction. But Med/Health Managers at 65% AI win rate shows that the administrative and strategic layer of healthcare is already vulnerable. HIPAA compliance slows but doesn't prevent this shift.
Industry Outlook — Where Human Work Gets Displaced First
Now Sales professionals ($258B combined) face immediate disruption. Sales Managers ($97B, 79% AI win rate) and Sales Reps Wholesale ($103B, 68%) have the highest win rates among large professions. AI CRM copilots handle prospecting, pipeline management, and follow-ups at superhuman levels. Expect 25-40% headcount reduction in transactional sales by 2028.
Now Software Developers ($239B, 70%) — AI wins 7 of 10 expert evaluations. AI coding agents (Claude Code, Codex, Cursor) are the displacement vector. The 30% human-advantage gap (complex architecture, debugging, domain expertise) may persist, but productivity per developer doubles — meaning fewer developers needed for the same output.
Now Editors ($8B, 75%) and Compliance Officers ($34B, 69%) face rapid per-capita disruption. Smaller compensation pools but among the highest AI win rates. AI generates, edits, and reviews content and regulatory documents at near-expert quality. These professions shrink fastest as a percentage of current workforce.
Next Gen/Ops Managers ($477B, 67%) — the mega displacement target. AI handles analytics, forecasting, reporting, and resource optimization. Physical operations management creates a partial moat. Augmentation accelerates to partial displacement by 2028-2029, with the knowledge-work component of the role increasingly automated.
Next Healthcare back-office ($119B contested zone) opens as AI capabilities improve. Med/Health Managers ($78B, 65%) and Nurse Practitioners ($41B, 56%) do partly automatable work behind physical care frontlines. HIPAA and clinical licensing slow but don't prevent adoption of AI-assisted diagnosis, scheduling, and administrative workflows.
Later Professional judgment roles ($392B combined) remain human-led for 5+ years. Accountants ($135B, 24%), Financial Managers ($148B, 32%), Lawyers ($137B, 46%) — all below the AI win rate midline. Regulatory complexity, professional standards, and client trust create durable moats. AI augments but doesn't displace.
Later Nurses ($323B, 37%), Pharmacists ($45B, 26%) remain human-dominated. Physical patient care, clinical judgment, and licensing barriers that no language model can breach. These professions face robotics disruption, not LLM disruption — on a 10-20 year horizon.
